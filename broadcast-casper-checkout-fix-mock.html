Weekly CASPer tip

Explain what you are doing, and why.

CASPer is designed to be an objective exam, as much as possible. Markers are likely told not to assume your rationale or motivation, because the rationale itself is part of what the exam is testing.

So phrases like "I would be non-confrontational" do very little on their own. The skill is showing how you would be non-confrontational, and why that matters in the situation.

Instead of naming a trait, describe the behaviour.
Private conversation, neutral language, asking before assuming, acknowledging emotions, clarifying facts, and explaining why this protects trust or safety.

This is not about memorising model answers. It is about making your reasoning visible enough that a marker can score the competency you are actually demonstrating.
