How Flinders selects for medicine

Flinders offers a graduate-entry Doctor of Medicine with a strong rural and remote focus. Selection combines your GPA, the GAMSAT, and a multiple mini interview (MMI) for shortlisted applicants.

Official source: Flinders Medicine admissions at flinders.edu.au.

What the Flinders MMI tends to reward

Flinders has a long history in rural, remote and Indigenous health, and in our coaching experience its MMI rewards candidates who reflect that authentically:

  • Genuine rural and remote interest - real, specific motivation rather than a rehearsed line.
  • Cultural awareness and respect when a station calls for it, without lecturing.
  • Warm, clear communication and visible empathy in role-play stations.
  • Balanced, honest reasoning.

Common mistakes we see

  • A metro-only outlook when the program is clearly rural and remote.
  • Generic answers with no specific connection to people or place.
  • Talking over the other person in a role-play.
  • Sounding rehearsed rather than genuine.

About the Flinders MD program

Flinders' Doctor of Medicine is a graduate-entry program of around four years, based in Adelaide with extensive rural and remote placements, including in the Northern Territory (Darwin). Teaching is integrated and case-based with early and immersive clinical exposure. Confirm the current structure with Flinders.

Fees (approximate)

Most domestic students study in a Commonwealth Supported Place (CSP) and pay the government-set annual student contribution for medicine - roughly AUD 10,000-11,000 per year as a 2025 guide, subject to change. Full-fee domestic and international places cost substantially more. For orientation only; confirm current fees with Flinders.

Research opportunities

Flinders offers research and honours-style options alongside the MD, with strengths in rural, remote and Indigenous health research. Confirm current options with Flinders.

What students say (general perception)

The points below summarise common themes in public student discussion - opinions, not facts, and your experience may differ. Form your own view via open days and current students.

  • Commonly praised: outstanding rural/remote clinical immersion and a strong sense of purpose.
  • Commonly raised as challenges: rural and NT placements that require relocation and adaptability.
