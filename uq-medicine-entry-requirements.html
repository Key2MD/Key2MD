University of Queensland · Graduate Medicine

UQ Medicine uses unweighted GAMSAT — and an Honours degree can override your GPA entirely.

UQ ranks by a 50:50 combination of key-degree GPA and unweighted GAMSAT. For applicants with Honours or a research higher degree, specific GPA values are deemed by UQ — which can completely change the competitive picture.

✓ Min GPA: 5.0 in key degree ✓ Min GAMSAT: 50 each section (unweighted) ★ Honours Class I → GPA 7.00 No CASPer required

MMI & Interview Lens

UQ MMI: early patient contact, learning communities and Queensland scale.

UQ's MD is one of Australia's largest and most research-integrated programs. MMI interviewers look for applicants who combine intellectual curiosity with patient-centred values — and who understand how to stay grounded in a large, complex health system.

Early patient contactUQ highlights integrated, clinically contextualised learning with early patient contact from the first year. Interviewers want to know what you've done with any existing patient exposure.
Learning community structureStudents join a learning community that acts as a clinical home throughout the program. Small-group learning within a large institution — knowing this is part of UQ's model demonstrates genuine research into the program.
Rural Clinical School scaleUQ Rural Clinical School is described as Australia's largest rural clinical school hosted by a single university, with sites at Bundaberg, Hervey Bay, Rockhampton, Toowoomba and more. Rural interest? Know the sites.
Ochsner and global clinical optionsUQ's partnership with Ochsner Health in New Orleans provides international clinical experience. A UQ-Ochsner pathway and Year 4 placement exist — relevant for research and global health-focused applicants.
Interview angle: Connect scale with humility at UQ — teamwork, intellectual drive, early patient contact, rural exposure and the discipline to keep patients at the centre of a complex, high-opportunity system.

The ranking formula: unweighted GAMSAT matters here

UQ uses the unweighted GAMSAT — a simple (1:1:1) average of Sections 1, 2 and 3. This is the opposite of Melbourne, which weights Section 3 double. The practical effect: if Section 3 is carrying your weighted GAMSAT score, UQ will show you a lower GAMSAT than Melbourne. If Sections 1 and 2 are strong relative to your S3, UQ may look better.

Interview selection formula

Rank = 50% × GPA score + 50% × unweighted GAMSAT
GPA = GEMSAS key-degree GPA (or deemed GPA if Honours/HDR applies) · GAMSAT = (S1 + S2 + S3) ÷ 3 · Each section minimum 50

UQ GPA overrides: Honours and higher degrees

UQ applies a deemed GPA to certain completed key degrees rather than using the GEMSAS calculated GPA. This rule applies only when the relevant qualification is the key degree UQ assesses. An Honours year attached to an undergraduate degree that is not the key degree does not automatically trigger these overrides.

7.00
Honours Class I (as key degree)
UQ deems a first-class Honours degree completed as the key degree to have GPA 7.00. This is the maximum possible GPA on the 7-point scale. For applicants with a lower undergraduate GPA, a first-class Honours can be transformative for UQ competitiveness.
Maximum GPA
7.00
AQF Level 10 Research Masters or Doctoral degree (as key degree)
A completed AQF Level 10 research masters degree or doctoral degree (PhD) used as the key degree is deemed GPA 7.00. This does not apply to coursework masters degrees — the degree must be research-based at AQF Level 10.
Maximum GPA
6.00
Honours Class IIA (as key degree)
UQ deems Honours Class IIA to have GPA 6.00. For applicants whose underlying GEMSAS GPA is below 6.0, this can meaningfully improve the 50:50 rank.
Above average
5.00
Honours Class IIB (as key degree)
UQ deems Honours Class IIB to have GPA 5.00. At this level, the override provides a floor but no uplift above strong undergraduate GPA performance.
Floor value
4.00
Honours Class III (as key degree)
UQ deems Honours Class III to have GPA 4.00. This is below the 5.0 minimum GPA for eligibility — applicants with Class III as the key degree are unlikely to meet the GPA hurdle on this basis alone.

Rural background adjustments at UQ

UQ states that rural-background applicants receive two adjustments to the unweighted GAMSAT score. The specific values for these adjustments are not published in the public GEMSAS guide in a clean numeric form — they are qualitative/structural benefits rather than a published combo increment. Rural applicants should treat this as a meaningful pathway advantage and verify the current specifics directly with GEMSAS.

What applicants most commonly get wrong at UQ

❌
Using the weighted GAMSAT score instead of unweighted
UQ uses the unweighted average (S1+S2+S3)÷3. If your S3 is much higher than S1 and S2, the weighted score looks better than the unweighted — and Melbourne will show a better GAMSAT than UQ does. Check which sections are your strongest before ordering preferences.
❌
Not knowing whether their Honours year is the key degree
The GPA override only applies when Honours or the research degree is the designated key degree UQ assesses. If Honours was completed as part of an undergraduate degree that is not the key degree, the override does not automatically apply. Check the GEMSAS guide on key degree designation.
❌
Thinking a coursework masters applies the PhD GPA override
The AQF Level 10 override applies to research masters and doctoral degrees only. A coursework masters (AQF Level 9) does not receive GPA 7.00. This is one of the most common confusions among postgraduate applicants at UQ.
❌
Overlooking the rural adjustment pathway
Rural-background adjustments exist at UQ but are not always visible to applicants who haven't carefully read the GEMSAS guide. If you have a genuine rural background, make sure it is documented properly in your GEMSAS application.
