University of Notre Dame Australia · Sydney Campus

Notre Dame Sydney uses GPA, GAMSAT, CASPer and bonus points — all equally weighted at 30%.

UNDS is one of only three Australian graduate-entry medical schools that uses CASPer formally in its selection formula. CASPer carries the same weight as GPA and GAMSAT — a strong CASPer can compensate for a lower academic score, and a weak CASPer can kill an otherwise competitive application.

MMI & Interview Lens

Notre Dame Sydney MMI: values, community and faith-based healthcare.

Notre Dame is a Catholic institution with a strong emphasis on human dignity, community service, ethics and social justice. The Sydney campus is not purely a "low-combo safety" school — the MMI weights values and interpersonal communication heavily, and the CASPer score (30% of your ranking) reflects exactly these qualities. Show service orientation, not just academic credentials.

Human dignity and ethicsNotre Dame's curriculum is anchored in Catholic intellectual tradition with a strong bioethics emphasis. MMI stations frequently probe your reasoning about ethical dilemmas, patient rights and the limits of medical intervention. Be prepared to reason through values-based scenarios with nuance.
Community service and social justiceThe program integrates community engagement and places high value on service orientation. Your CASPer responses and MMI answers should demonstrate genuine prior engagement with others — volunteering, advocacy, or professional work in service contexts.
Clinical breadth — Darlinghurst, Blacktown and beyondUNDS students train across a network of Sydney clinical schools. Awareness of diverse patient populations — from inner-city complex presentations at St Vincent's to Western Sydney community health — positions you as clinically contextually aware.
Rural and Indigenous healthNotre Dame places rural and Indigenous health prominently in its curriculum. If you have relevant rural or Indigenous health experience, or a genuine interest in health equity, this is a school where that matters — and should be articulated clearly in your CASPer, MMI and portfolio.
Interview angle: Notre Dame Sydney values applicants who understand medicine as a vocation, not just a career. Frame your motivations in terms of service, patient-centred care and health equity. Avoid purely career-focused language — show you understand why medicine at a values-driven institution looks different from medicine at a research-intensive university.

Interview selection formula

UNDS ranks applicants for interview using a 30:30:30:10 standardised combination of unweighted average GAMSAT, GPA, CASPer and bonus points. All four components are standardised (z-scored) before combining — meaning your relative position in each component matters, not just your raw score. Notre Dame is one of only three Australian graduate-entry schools (alongside UNDF and UOW) that formally weights CASPer in its selection formula.

Offer ranking formula

Once you are invited to interview and complete the online MMI, offers are made based on a 50:50 combination of your pre-interview composite score and your interview score. The pre-interview composite is the same 30:30:30:10 formula described above.

50%
Pre-interview composite
The same 30:30:30:10 score: GAMSAT + GPA + CASPer + bonus points. Your academic and CASPer profile carries through to final offers.
50%
MMI interview
Online Multi-Mini Interview with at least 7 stations. Each station has a single interviewer. Recorded using online interviewing software.
CASPer carries equal weight to GPA and GAMSAT. This is a fundamental difference from most graduate-entry schools. A Q4 CASPer score can materially improve your composite ranking relative to applicants with the same GPA and GAMSAT. Treating CASPer as an afterthought is the most common strategic mistake at Notre Dame.

Minimum entry requirements

1
Undergraduate degree — AQF Level 7 minimum
A completed bachelor's degree at AQF Level 7 or above. Study must be from a recognised institution. UNDS also considers professional experience and the Notre Dame Portfolio in the context of your application.
2
Minimum GPA: 5.2 (GEMSAS weighted)
The GEMSAS-calculated weighted GPA must be at least 5.2. This is higher than most schools' minimum. Note that not-for-degree study is excluded from the GPA calculation. The competitive GPA for interview offers is typically well above 5.2 — the 2023 cohort averaged 6.7.
3
GAMSAT: ≥52 unweighted average, ≥50 each section
Notre Dame uses the unweighted average of the three GAMSAT sections — meaning S1, S2, and S3 are averaged without the 2:2:3 weighting used by most other schools. The minimum overall is 52 (not 50) and each section must be at least 50. This distinction is important: your unweighted average may differ from your ACER overall weighted score.
4
CASPer — mandatory, no published minimum
All applicants must sit CASPer. There is no published minimum score, but CASPer is 30% of your interview ranking. Notre Dame is one of the few schools where a genuinely excellent CASPer can make an application competitive despite a GPA or GAMSAT that is just above the minimum.

Bonus points

Notre Dame Sydney awards bonus points as the 10% component of the ranking formula. The exact numerical values are not published, but the qualifying categories are confirmed in the GEMSAS guide. Bonus points are applied consistently to both interview selection and offer ranking.

Bonus
Rural background
Applicants with demonstrated rural background receive bonus points. Evidence of rurality is required — check the GEMSAS guide for the required documentation standard. The exact point value is not published.

Facilitated interview pathways

Notre Dame Sydney offers facilitated interview pathways (FIPs) for specific undergraduate graduates. FIP applicants must still meet all standard GEMSAS entry requirements, and must list Notre Dame Sydney as their first preference, along with meeting the FIP-specific minimum thresholds.

Notre Dame undergraduate degree graduates
Graduates of any Notre Dame undergraduate degree who were not otherwise offered an interview through the standard GEMSAS process may be considered through the FIP. This includes both Sydney and Fremantle campus UNDA undergraduate graduates.
Requirements: GPA ≥6.0, unweighted GAMSAT ≥55; UNDS listed as first preference; completed within current or preceding year.
ACU Bachelor of Biomedical Sciences (on-campus)
Applicants completing the ACU BBS on-campus (not distance/online) at ACU who meet the FIP thresholds may be considered for a facilitated interview at Notre Dame Sydney. The ACU BBS pathway is specific to the Sydney campus only.
Requirements: GPA ≥6.0, unweighted GAMSAT ≥55; UNDS listed as first preference; completed within current or preceding year.
FIP does not bypass the standard selection process. FIP applicants are still ranked against each other using the standard formula. The pathway provides a structured opportunity for eligible graduates who narrowly missed an interview in the standard pool — it does not guarantee an interview.

What applicants most commonly get wrong at Notre Dame Sydney

❌
Treating CASPer as an afterthought
CASPer is 30% of your interview ranking — the same weight as GPA and GAMSAT. Sitting CASPer with no preparation and hoping your academic scores carry you is a real and common strategic mistake at UNDS. Invest at least 4–6 weeks in CASPer preparation before your test date.
❌
Using the weighted GAMSAT (instead of unweighted average)
Notre Dame uses the unweighted average of S1, S2, S3. ACER's overall score uses a 2:2:3 (S1:S2:S3) weighting. If your S3 is particularly strong, your ACER overall score will be higher than your unweighted average — meaning you're less competitive at Notre Dame than your ACER score suggests.
❌
Not checking if not-for-degree study affects your GPA
UNDS (and UNDF) exclude not-for-degree study from GPA calculations. If you completed exchange units, cross-institutional subjects, or other not-for-degree study, these are excluded. This may meaningfully change your calculated GPA compared to other schools.
❌
Thinking it's a "low-combo safety" — UNDS is not easy to get into
The 2023 cohort averaged 6.7 GPA and 66 GAMSAT. UNDS is genuinely competitive. The minimum 5.2 GPA is an eligibility floor, not a competitive benchmark. The inclusion of CASPer adds a dimension of variability that makes the school both accessible for strong CASPer performers and competitive overall.
❌
Missing the HDR bonus deadline
HDR bonus points require your Masters by Research or PhD to be completed — conferred — by 31 July in the application year. An in-progress HDR, even one submitting in August, does not qualify. Plan your submission and examination timeline carefully if this is relevant to you.
