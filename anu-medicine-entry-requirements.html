Australian National University

ANU Medicine rewards strong GPA/GAMSAT plus selected higher degrees.

ANU uses weighted GPA and overall GAMSAT, with a non-stacking percentage bonus for eligible standalone Honours, Masters by Research or PhD qualifications.

MMI lens

ANU MMI talking points: research, systems and Canberra clinical context.

ANU presents the MChD as a research-rich medical program built around medical sciences, clinical skills, population health, professionalism and leadership. For MMI prep, the key is to show evidence-literate judgement, not just interest in a high-ranking university.

Research from day oneANU requires MChD students to complete a research project drawing on ANU and ACT/Commonwealth research strengths.
Patient-centred care under uncertaintyLearning outcomes emphasise evidence-based medicine, ethical reasoning, teamwork and patient-centred decision-making.
Rural, First Nations and social medicineThe curriculum highlights social foundations of medicine, First Nations health context and rural or remote Australian healthcare.
Global connectionANU explicitly links health care to international health, human rights, globalisation and respect for cultural difference.
Interview angle: In an ANU interview, talk less about prestige and more about how a research-intensive, policy-adjacent Canberra environment would help you become a careful, evidence-literate clinician.

What Students Miss

ANU bonuses apply to the combined GPA/GAMSAT score after minimum requirements are met. They are helpful, but they are not additive across multiple degrees.
