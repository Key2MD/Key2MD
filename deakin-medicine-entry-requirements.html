Deakin Medicine: adjustment factors can move the needle more than GAMSAT.

Deakin ranks the general stream by combined GPA/GAMSAT aggregate — but then applies percentage adjustments for work experience, rurality, Deakin study and financial disadvantage. For some applicants, these shifts the read completely.

MMI & Interview Lens

Deakin MMI: rural medicine, cultural safety, and social accountability.

Deakin frames its MD explicitly around rural and regional medicine, Indigenous health, public health, ethics and service to marginalised communities. Interviewers expect genuine engagement with what this curriculum demands — not a generic statement about wanting to help people.

Rural and regional identityDeakin says the course has a unique focus on rural and regional medicine and aims to address doctor shortages in regional areas. Know why this matters to you personally.
Indigenous health and marginalised communitiesThe MD foregrounds Indigenous health, public health, ethics, law and service to marginalised communities. Deakin interviewers probe cultural safety and social determinants awareness.
Early and distributed clinical exposureStudents begin in simulated environments before rotating through clinical school sites at Geelong, Eastern Health (Melbourne), Ballarat, Warrnambool and Rural Communities clinical schools.
Rural Community Clinical SchoolExtended community-based care with a clinical mentor through placements in smaller rural and regional towns. The school values doctors who can build long-term community relationships.
Interview angle: Deakin interviewers want to see that rural placements, resource constraints, continuity of care and culturally safe communication shape your actual understanding of medicine — not just that you ticked "rural" in your application.

How Deakin ranks applicants for interview

Unlike UOW, Deakin does not use CASPer and does not have a combo hurdle above the GPA/GAMSAT minimums. The general stream ranks all eligible applicants by their adjusted aggregate score. A higher combined score — after applicable adjustment factors — earns an interview offer.

Combined aggregate formula

Adjusted aggregate = (GPA/7 + GAMSAT/100) × (1 + adjustment %)
GPA = GEMSAS weighted GPA · GAMSAT = unweighted overall score · Adjustment % = sum of all eligible Deakin adjustment factors (no published cap)

The GAMSAT used is the unweighted overall score — the simple average of Sections 1, 2 and 3. This differs from Melbourne, which uses a 1:1:2 weighted GAMSAT. If Section 3 is your strongest section, Deakin may give you a lower GAMSAT than weighted-score schools.

A student with a 1.67 base combo and two adjustment factors (Deakin study + rural residence) can end up ahead of a student with a 1.74 raw combo and no adjustments.

Rural Training Stream

Deakin has a separate Rural Training Stream with its own ranking process. This stream is for applicants committed to rural medical practice and training. The application for the Rural Training Stream includes written application elements — meaning academic scores alone do not determine outcomes in this stream as cleanly as in the general stream.

Rural applicants should check the Rural Training Stream rules directly on the Deakin Doctor of Medicine website, because the written application components can matter significantly in addition to the aggregate score.

Clinical schools and training locations

Deakin distributes clinical training across multiple sites in Victoria. Understanding where training occurs is useful both for your application decision and for MMI conversations about rural medicine and community-engaged healthcare.

Major clinical school sites

Geelong (Barwon Health) Eastern Health (Melbourne) Ballarat (Grampians) Warrnambool Rural Communities Clinical School Rural Community Clinical School (extended placements)

The Rural Community Clinical School places students with a clinical mentor in smaller rural and regional towns for extended periods. This is distinct from shorter rural rotations and represents a significant portion of training for students who select this stream.

What applicants most commonly get wrong at Deakin

❌
Treating Deakin like a pure combo school
The biggest mistake. A student with a 1.68 raw combo and +8% rural plus +4% clinical adjustment ends up with an adjusted aggregate roughly equivalent to a 1.82 raw combo. Adjustments are substantial — not cosmetic.
❌
Claiming both clinical and work experience adjustments
These are mutually exclusive. If you have eligible clinical experience (12 months FTE as a registered health professional), claim that instead of the general work experience adjustment — it's worth twice as much (+4% vs +2%).
❌
Not knowing the Deakin GAMSAT formula
Deakin uses the unweighted GAMSAT (simple average of S1, S2, S3). If Section 3 is your strength, you may look stronger at Melbourne (1:1:2 weighting) than at Deakin. This should factor into your preference ordering.
❌
Applying for the Rural Training Stream without preparing written application materials
The Rural Training Stream has written application elements. Applicants who do not engage seriously with these components can be ranked below others with lower aggregate scores who prepared thorough written responses.
