How ANU selects for medicine

ANU offers a graduate-entry medicine program in Canberra. Selection combines your GPA, the GAMSAT, and a multiple mini interview (MMI) for shortlisted applicants.

Official source: ANU Medicine admissions at anu.edu.au.

What the ANU MMI tends to reward

In our coaching experience, ANU's MMI rewards candidates who think out loud like a thoughtful future clinician:

  • Reasoned argument under challenge - you hold your line where it is sound and adjust gracefully where it is not.
  • Awareness of population and rural health when a station invites it, without lecturing.
  • Genuine, considered motivation for medicine as a graduate.
  • Composure and clarity under time pressure.

Common mistakes we see

  • Ignoring the wider population or equity context a station is hinting at.
  • Generic answers that could apply to any applicant.
  • Defending a first answer instead of thinking aloud when pushed.
  • Sounding rehearsed rather than genuine.

About the ANU Medicine program

ANU's graduate-entry program runs around four years, leading to a Doctor of Medicine and Surgery, based in Canberra with placements across the ACT and surrounding rural and regional areas. As a research-intensive university, the curriculum carries a strong academic and population-health emphasis. Confirm the current structure with ANU.

Fees (approximate)

Most domestic students study in a Commonwealth Supported Place (CSP) and pay the government-set annual student contribution for medicine - roughly AUD 10,000-11,000 per year as a 2025 guide, subject to change. Full-fee domestic and international places cost substantially more. For orientation only; confirm current fees with ANU.

Research opportunities

ANU's research strength is a drawcard: there are strong research components and pathways within and alongside the program, with links to its medical research schools. Confirm current options with ANU.

What students say (general perception)

The points below summarise common themes in public student discussion - opinions, not facts, and your experience may differ. Form your own view via open days and current students.

  • Commonly praised: research opportunities, a smaller cohort, and Canberra's manageable pace.
  • Commonly raised as challenges: Canberra being a smaller city, and relocation for those from elsewhere.
