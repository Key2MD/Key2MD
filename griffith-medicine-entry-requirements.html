Griffith Medicine: a completed PhD converts to GPA 7.0 — the maximum possible.

Griffith uses unweighted GPA and overall GAMSAT for ranking. A completed PhD is deemed GPA 7.0. Masters by Research has a component GPA treatment. Rural-background applicants access priority entry.

MMI & Interview Lens

Griffith MMI: social equity, community medicine and Southeast Queensland scale.

Griffith frames its MD around social accountability, community engagement and interprofessional practice. The Gold Coast and Logan campuses serve diverse communities — interviewers look for applicants who understand health equity, not just clinical excellence.

Social accountability and equityGriffith's MD foregrounds social accountability and commitment to underserved communities. Know what social determinants of health mean for Southeast Queensland populations.
Community-based and interprofessional learningStudents learn alongside nursing, pharmacy and other health professional students. Interprofessional collaboration is embedded — understand what working in a team of non-doctor health professionals requires.
Gold Coast and Logan clinical settingsTraining spans Gold Coast University Hospital, Logan Hospital and community health settings across Southeast Queensland. Urban, suburban and regional diversity is real — engage with it.
Rural and remote emphasisGriffith actively recruits rural-background students and has a rural priority pathway. If you have rural experience, articulate what rural healthcare actually demands beyond city-based training.
Interview angle: Griffith interviewers value social accountability and community engagement over prestige. Show how you would contribute to the health of underserved populations — not just how you would excel in a hospital setting.

Ranking at Griffith: GPA/GAMSAT aggregate

Griffith ranks applicants by their combined GPA/GAMSAT aggregate using unweighted GPA (unlike Melbourne's recency weighting). There is no published single hard GAMSAT cutoff beyond the 50-per-section minimum — the practical question is where your total combined score ranks against the applicant pool.

Griffith also does not publish a strict minimum section-by-section GAMSAT number beyond 50. The overall score and GPA combination determines competitiveness. Rural applicants are ranked in a separate priority pool.

Rural priority access

Griffith has a rural priority pathway that provides access advantages for applicants with genuine rural or remote background. Rural applicants are considered in a separate ranking pool. The practical effect is that rural applicants can be competitive at a lower absolute GPA/GAMSAT combination than the general stream — but priority access is not a guarantee.

What applicants most commonly get wrong at Griffith

❌
Assuming the Masters by Research gives a clean GPA 7.0
The Masters by Research treatment at Griffith depends on the degree structure. Only the research component (FTE equivalent) may receive GPA 7.0 — the full effect depends on how much of the degree was research-based. This is shown as qualitative in the calculator because a precise number cannot be calculated without degree-specific details.
❌
Not registering as a rural applicant if eligible
Griffith's rural priority pathway places rural applicants in a separate ranking pool. Rural applicants who don't identify their background correctly in the GEMSAS application don't access this advantage. If you have a genuine rural background, document it properly.
❌
Looking for a published minimum GAMSAT cutoff
Griffith does not publish a single hard GAMSAT cutoff beyond the 50-per-section eligibility minimum. The competitive cutoff is set each year by the applicant pool. This means Griffith's effective cutoff varies more year-to-year than schools with a published minimum above 50.
