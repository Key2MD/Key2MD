University of Western Australia · Graduate Medicine

UWA Medicine has the highest GAMSAT floor of any GEMSAS school — and weights GPA at 60%.

UWA requires a minimum GAMSAT of 55, making it a harder eligibility hurdle than most GEMSAS schools. Beyond the hurdle, GPA carries 60% weight in interview ranking. PhD applicants receive a deemed GPA 7.0 — a significant competitive advantage.

✓ Min GPA: 5.5 ⚠ Min GAMSAT: 55 overall (higher than most schools) 60% GPA + 40% GAMSAT ranking ★ PhD = deemed GPA 7.0

MMI & Interview Lens

UWA MMI: patient wellbeing, WA health context and the six-role curriculum.

UWA frames its MD around patient, community and society wellbeing, structured around six roles: professional, leader, advocate, clinician, educator and scholar. MMI interviewers expect candidates to understand what each role demands — not just the clinical one.

Six-role curriculumUWA's MD is explicitly structured around professional, leader, advocate, clinician, educator and scholar roles. Know what advocacy and leadership look like for a doctor — not just clinical skills.
Perth and community clinical exposureHospital, general practice and community attachments across Perth, plus final-year elective, selective and internship-preparation blocks. WA's health system context — distance, equity, rural access — should feature in your answers.
Rural Clinical School WAIn Year 3, a quarter of the cohort studies in rural WA, with settings from Esperance to Kununurra. Rural WA training is a major structural commitment — understand what it asks of students.
Research ecosystemUWA highlights clinical mentoring, QEII-linked facilities and research connections including the Lions Eye Institute, Harry Perkins Institute, PathWest, Perron Institute and The Kids Research Institute Australia.
Interview angle: Connect your motivation to WA health realities: distance, rural access, mentoring culture, teamwork and the responsibility to serve patient and community wellbeing in a unique geographic context.

The GAMSAT hurdle: UWA's most important eligibility gate

UWA's published minimum GAMSAT is 55 overall, with no individual section below 50. This is significantly higher than the typical 50 floor at most GEMSAS schools. A student with a GAMSAT of 53 or 54 may be competitive at Deakin, Griffith or ANU but ineligible at UWA.

Check eligibility before preferencing UWA. The 55 GAMSAT minimum is a hard floor. Applicants who do not meet this threshold are not ranked, regardless of GPA strength.

Interview selection ranking: GPA dominates

Once both hurdles are met, UWA ranks applicants for interview using a 60:40 GPA/GAMSAT formula. This is GPA-heavy relative to most GEMSAS schools — a strong GPA advantage matters more here than at schools using 50:50 or combo-based approaches.

Overall GAMSAT

UWA uses the overall GAMSAT score — the ACER-reported weighted score — rather than the unweighted section average used by Melbourne and UQ. If your S3 is strong relative to S1 and S2, UWA's GAMSAT score will look better than Melbourne's equivalent.

Higher-degree GPA rules at UWA

UWA has specific GPA treatments for research degrees that can meaningfully change your competitive position — especially if your undergraduate GPA was modest.

QualificationUWA GPA treatmentNotes
Completed PhDDeemed GPA 7.0PhD from a recognised institution by time of application. This gives the maximum possible GPA on the 7-point scale.
Completed UWA Doctor of Dental Medicine (DMD)Deemed GPA 7.0UWA DMD graduates receive the same deemed GPA treatment as PhD graduates. Unique to UWA.
Completed Masters by Research+0.2 added to GPACompleted by 31 July (application year). A modest bonus — adds 0.2 to the GPA used for ranking, capped by the 7.0 scale ceiling.

Rural offer ranking

Rural applicants at UWA have their rural rating incorporated into offer ranking. This operates as a practical advantage for rural-background applicants who clear the eligibility hurdles — rural classification changes how you are ranked, not just whether you are eligible.

What applicants most commonly get wrong at UWA

❌
Not checking the GAMSAT minimum before preferencing UWA
The 55 GAMSAT hurdle is the most common eligibility trap at UWA. Applicants who focus only on the GPA side of their profile — or who reference the 50 minimum used by most other GEMSAS schools — can be caught out. Check your GAMSAT against 55, not 50.
❌
Thinking a coursework masters earns the PhD GPA bonus
The +0.2 GPA bonus applies to a completed Masters by Research — a thesis-based research degree. A coursework masters does not receive any GPA adjustment at UWA. The PhD (GPA 7.0) and DMD (GPA 7.0) rules require those specific qualifications.
❌
Underweighting the importance of GPA in UWA's formula
UWA uses 60% GPA and 40% GAMSAT for interview selection. This means GPA differences have a proportionally larger impact here than at schools using 50:50. If you have a strong GAMSAT but moderate GPA, UWA may show as less competitive than other schools — and vice versa.
