University of Western Australia

UWA Medicine weights GPA heavily before interview.

UWA uses overall GAMSAT and GPA for interview selection, with a 60% GPA and 40% GAMSAT balance before interview.

MMI lens

UWA MMI talking points: patient wellbeing, mentorship and WA clinical breadth.

UWA frames its MD around patient, community and society wellbeing, with doctors as professionals, leaders, advocates, clinicians, educators and scholars. For MMI, show that you understand WA context, rural distance and scholarly practice.

Six-role curriculumUWA says its MD is structured around the roles of professional, leader, advocate, clinician, educator and scholar.
Perth and community clinical exposureStudents undertake hospital, general practice and community attachments, plus final-year elective, selective and internship-preparation blocks.
Rural Clinical School WAIn year 3, a quarter of the cohort studies in rural WA, with settings from Esperance to Kununurra.
Mentorship and research ecosystemUWA highlights clinical mentoring, QEII-linked facilities and research connections including the Lions Eye Institute, Harry Perkins Institute, PathWest, Perron Institute and The Kids Research Institute Australia.
Interview angle: For UWA, connect your motivation to WA health needs: distance, rural access, mentoring, teamwork and the responsibility to serve patient and community wellbeing.

What Students Miss

UWA has a higher GAMSAT eligibility hurdle than many GEMSAS schools. A student with strong GPA but a 53 or 54 overall GAMSAT may look good elsewhere but not clear UWA's published hurdle.
