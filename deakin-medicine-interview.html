How Deakin selects for medicine

Deakin offers a graduate-entry Doctor of Medicine with a strong rural and regional focus. Selection combines your GPA, the GAMSAT, and a multiple mini interview (MMI) for shortlisted applicants.

Official source: Deakin Medicine admissions at deakin.edu.au.

What the Deakin MMI tends to reward

Deakin trains many doctors for regional Victoria, and in our coaching experience its MMI rewards candidates who fit that mission genuinely:

  • Authentic regional and rural interest - specific, real reasons rather than a rehearsed line.
  • Team-minded communication - you work with people and explain clearly.
  • Empathy you can hear in role-play stations.
  • Balanced, honest reasoning rather than a rush to the "right" answer.

Common mistakes we see

  • A metro-only outlook when the program is clearly regional.
  • Generic answers with no specific connection to people or place.
  • Talking over the other person in a role-play.
  • Sounding rehearsed rather than genuine.

About the Deakin MD program

Deakin's Doctor of Medicine is a graduate-entry program of around four years, based in Geelong with extensive regional and rural placements across Victoria (including sites such as Warrnambool). Teaching is integrated and case-based with early clinical exposure. Confirm the current structure with Deakin.

Fees (approximate)

Most domestic students study in a Commonwealth Supported Place (CSP) and pay the government-set annual student contribution for medicine - roughly AUD 10,000-11,000 per year as a 2025 guide, subject to change. Full-fee domestic and international places cost substantially more. For orientation only; confirm current fees with Deakin.

Research opportunities

Deakin offers research and honours-style options alongside the MD, with strengths in rural, regional and population health. Confirm current options with Deakin.

What students say (general perception)

The points below summarise common themes in public student discussion - opinions, not facts, and your experience may differ. Form your own view via open days and current students.

  • Commonly praised: strong rural/regional clinical exposure and a supportive, community-minded cohort.
  • Commonly raised as challenges: a Geelong base and rural placements that mean relocation for some.
