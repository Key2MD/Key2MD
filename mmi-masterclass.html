What it does

Turns MMI advice into examiner logic.

Most MMI prep tells students to be empathetic, structured, and reflective. This session shows what that actually sounds like when a station is ambiguous, emotionally loaded, or time pressured.

Examiner expectationsWhat assessors are listening for beneath the words: safety, maturity, emotional intelligence, judgement, and realism.
Common mistakesThe errors that make otherwise capable candidates sound generic, defensive, performative, or unsafe.
Authentic communicationHow to express warmth and emotion without sounding fake, theatrical, or over-rehearsed.
Station coverageEthics, role-play, teamwork, conflict, policy, personal reflection, and the broad “what would you do?” stations.
Built for repeated action. The goal is that you leave knowing how to think in the room, not just what phrases to memorise the night before.
