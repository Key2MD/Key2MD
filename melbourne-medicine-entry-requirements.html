University of Melbourne · Graduate Medicine

Melbourne MD: the interview is worth 50% of your final offer. Get the formula right.

Melbourne uses unweighted GAMSAT and a recency-weighted GPA to rank interview offers. Once you reach the interview, the MMI score dominates everything — worth 50% of your final place offer. This is one of Australia's most competitive medical programs.

MMI & Interview Lens

Melbourne MMI: intellectual breadth, complexity and clinical-school diversity.

Melbourne's MD spans clinical sciences, biomedical sciences, health systems and research. The MMI format tests your reasoning, ethics, communication and self-awareness — in an environment that attracts some of Australia's highest-calibre applicants. Strong academic scores get you to the interview. The MMI determines your offer.

Breadth of clinical schoolsMelbourne trains students across Royal Melbourne, St Vincent's, Western Health, Austin, Peter MacCallum, Royal Children's Hospital and rural clinical schools. Understanding this distribution signals genuine interest in the program.
Research integrationMelbourne's MD includes a significant research component. Applicants with research experience — even Honours or a PhD — should be able to articulate how research thinking benefits clinical practice.
Rural MD PathwayMelbourne has a dedicated MD Rural Pathway for students committed to rural medicine. If rural health is a genuine interest, know what Melbourne's rural training actually looks like before the interview.
Complexity and systems thinkingMelbourne interviewers expect applicants to handle ambiguity, systemic complexity and competing stakeholder interests. Rote answers and scripted responses are visible to experienced MMI assessors.
Interview angle: Melbourne rewards intellectual breadth and self-awareness. Show how you would use the clinical school diversity, research access and curriculum choice to become genuinely useful in complex health systems — not just how impressive your CV is.

The two-stage formula: interview selection then place offers

Melbourne has two distinct ranking stages. The formula changes significantly between them, and the MMI becomes far more decisive after interview.

Stage 1 — Who gets an interview offer

For the standard GEMSAS pathway, Melbourne selects interview candidates using GPA (recency-weighted) and equally weighted GAMSAT sections. The exact weighting for interview selection is not published as a simple percentage in the way Deakin or ANU are — applicants are effectively ranked by their combined academic profile.

This is the dominant factor after interview. Strong academic candidates with average MMI performance lose places to weaker academic profiles with strong interviews.

GPA calculation: Melbourne's recency weighting

Melbourne does not use the raw GEMSAS GPA. Instead, it applies recency weighting that emphasises later years of study. The final two years of an undergraduate degree carry more weight than earlier years.

The practical implication: an upward GPA trajectory — where your best results are in the most recent years — is rewarded at Melbourne relative to a flat or declining performance. If your final two years were significantly stronger than your early undergraduate years, Melbourne may rate you higher than a raw GPA calculation suggests.

GAMSAT at Melbourne: unweighted sections matter

Melbourne uses equally weighted GAMSAT sections — a simple 1:1:1 average of S1, S2 and S3. This means the ACER weighted score (which gives S3 double weight) is not what Melbourne uses.

If your S3 is much higher than S1 and S2, the ACER weighted score looks better than Melbourne's calculation. Conversely, if S1 and S2 are your strong sections relative to S3, Melbourne may rate your GAMSAT higher than your ACER overall score suggests. Always compute what Melbourne sees, not just what ACER reports.

Special pathways and reranking schemes

Equity pathway
Graduate Access Melbourne (GAM)
Eligible applicants with documented sustained disadvantage may be reranked based on the level of their disadvantage. The GAM pool operates separately from the competitive standard ranking. Disadvantage must be genuine, documented and sustained — not temporary or minor. If you believe you qualify, review the GAM eligibility criteria carefully and apply formally.
Rural pathway
MD Rural Pathway
Melbourne has dedicated rural access arrangements and an MD Rural Pathway for applicants committed to rural medicine. Rural applicants may access priority access arrangements. Check the Melbourne MD website for current rural pathway rules, as these can change between admissions cycles.
Research pathway
High-performing cognate PhD
A small number of applicants with high-level performance in a recent cognate PhD may be reranked in their favour under strict quota limits. This is not a broad advantage — it applies to a specific, limited subset and is not guaranteed simply by having a PhD.
Indigenous pathway
Priority access for eligible Indigenous applicants
Melbourne provides priority access for eligible Indigenous applicants. Check the GEMSAS guide and the Melbourne MD website for current eligibility criteria and application requirements for this pathway.

What applicants most commonly get wrong at Melbourne

❌
Using ACER's weighted GAMSAT overall score instead of the unweighted
The ACER overall score is (S1+S2+S3×2)÷4. Melbourne uses (S1+S2+S3)÷3. If S3 is your highest section, Melbourne's GAMSAT will be lower than the ACER report shows. Always calculate the unweighted figure when assessing Melbourne competitiveness.
❌
Underestimating how much the MMI matters
50% of your place offer is the MMI. Every year, applicants with extremely strong academic profiles lose offers to candidates with lower academic scores who performed better in the MMI. If you've earned an interview at Melbourne, treat MMI preparation as the highest priority in the weeks before it.
❌
Not knowing how Melbourne's GPA weighting works
Melbourne's final-year weighting (x2) means applicants who performed better in later undergraduate years may be stronger at Melbourne than at other schools. If you have an upward trajectory, Melbourne's weighted GPA will reflect this — the calculator uses this weighting.
❌
Not applying for GAM if there's genuine documented disadvantage
Graduate Access Melbourne is a formal reranking mechanism. If you have sustained documented disadvantage that affected your academic performance, applying for GAM is worth investigating. Many applicants are unaware it exists or assume it won't apply to them.
