How Notre Dame selects for medicine

The University of Notre Dame Australia offers a graduate-entry Doctor of Medicine at its Sydney and Fremantle schools. Selection typically considers academic results, the GAMSAT, a written application or portfolio, and an interview, with the university's values and ethos central to the process.

Official source: Notre Dame Medicine admissions at notredame.edu.au.

What the Notre Dame interview tends to reward

Notre Dame places real weight on character and values, and in our coaching experience its interview rewards:

  • A genuine service ethos - real reasons for wanting to care for people, not slogans.
  • Empathy and warmth you can hear in how you speak.
  • Honest reflection on experiences that shaped you.
  • Clear communication and balanced judgement.

Common mistakes we see

  • Polished but hollow answers that do not reveal who you are.
  • Reciting values rather than showing them through real examples.
  • Not engaging with the person in front of you in role-play or conversation.
  • Generic motivation that could apply to any applicant or school.

About the Notre Dame MD program

Notre Dame's Doctor of Medicine is a graduate-entry program of around four years, delivered at its Sydney and Fremantle schools with a strong emphasis on ethics, communication and community/regional placements. Teaching is integrated and patient-centred. Confirm the current structure with Notre Dame.

Fees (approximate)

Notre Dame's medicine fees depend on the place type, which may include Commonwealth Supported and/or full-fee places. Because this varies and changes each year, we do not quote a figure here - confirm current fees, place types and any funding directly with Notre Dame.

Research opportunities

Notre Dame offers research and scholarly options alongside the MD. Confirm current options with Notre Dame.

What students say (general perception)

The points below summarise common themes in public student discussion - opinions, not facts, and your experience may differ. Form your own view via open days and current students.

  • Commonly praised: a supportive, values-driven culture and strong communication and ethics teaching.
  • Commonly raised as challenges: fee structure considerations and the values-based fit not suiting everyone.
