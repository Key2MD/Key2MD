Notre Dame Fremantle

Notre Dame Fremantle combines GPA, GAMSAT, CASPer and bonus points.

UNDF uses the same broad 30:30:30:10 interview-ranking structure as Sydney, with Fremantle-specific bonus considerations including WA residency.

MMI lens

Notre Dame Fremantle MMI talking points: social justice, rural medicine and practical formation.

Notre Dame Fremantle frames medicine through clinical experience, bioethics, advocacy, social justice and responsibility to improve healthcare outcomes. For MMI, this is a values-and-service school, not just a GPA/GAMSAT/CASPer pathway.

Bioethics and social justiceThe Fremantle School says its curriculum embeds bioethics, advocacy and social justice.
Small-group PBLKey features include small group teaching and problem-based learning, combined with rich clinical experiences across the program.
Aboriginal and preventive healthEmbedded learning spans research, professional development, population and preventive health and Aboriginal Health.
WA clinical reachNotre Dame material describes practical clinical training across varied settings, with rural and remote training opportunities and elective possibilities.
Interview angle: For Notre Dame Fremantle, frame answers around service, ethical judgement, advocacy and what it means to learn medicine across metro, rural and remote WA contexts.

Fremantle-Specific Bonus Flags

Fremantle applicants should check rurality, Western Australian residency, completed higher degrees by research, and any relevant UNDA facilitated interview pathway. KCRMT/Broome preferences have additional considerations and should be checked directly.

What Students Miss

UNDF can be a real opportunity for applicants with strong CASPer and relevant bonus points. But a low CASPer can hurt badly because it is not a minor side criterion.
