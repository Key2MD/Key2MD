University of Notre Dame Australia · Fremantle Campus

Notre Dame Fremantle: CASPer, WA residency bonus, and Australia's most remote full MD program at KCRMT Broome.

UNDF uses the same 30:30:30:10 formula as Notre Dame Sydney, with Fremantle-specific bonus considerations: Western Australian residency (10+ cumulative years) and the unique KCRMT Broome rural training pathway — Australia's first fully regionally based medical school.

MMI & Interview Lens

Notre Dame Fremantle MMI: social justice, rural medicine and WA community health.

Notre Dame Fremantle is a values-driven Catholic institution with a strong focus on bioethics, social justice, rural and remote medicine, and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander health. The Fremantle campus has a distinctly WA character — the KCRMT program, rural training stream, and WA residency bonus all signal that UNDF is deeply invested in the WA health workforce. MMI answers should reflect genuine engagement with these values, not just academic credentials.

Bioethics and social justiceThe Fremantle School explicitly embeds bioethics, advocacy, and social justice in its curriculum. Demonstrate your capacity to reason through complex ethical situations — especially those involving patients with limited resources, cultural differences, or challenging social circumstances.
Rural and remote medicine — WA contextFremantle's commitment to rural WA health is structural, not aspirational. The KCRMT Broome program is one expression of this. In MMI, show that you understand what rural and remote practice in WA actually involves — geographic isolation, resource constraints, cultural competence with First Nations communities.
Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander healthPopulation health, preventive medicine and Aboriginal health are integrated throughout the program. This is especially salient at Fremantle given WA's significant First Nations population. If you have relevant community engagement or cultural safety training, this is the school to articulate it.
Small-group and problem-based learningThe Fremantle curriculum uses small-group teaching and problem-based learning alongside clinical placements. MMI stations often assess collaborative reasoning and interpersonal communication — skills that directly map to PBL settings.
Interview angle: For UNDF, frame answers around service, ethical judgement, and what it means to practice medicine across metro, rural and remote WA contexts. If you have WA connections, rural background, or First Nations health engagement — use them. UNDF is a school where context matters as much as academic credentials.

Interview selection formula

UNDF ranks applicants for interview using a 30:30:30:10 standardised combination of unweighted average GAMSAT, GPA, CASPer and bonus points — identical in structure to Notre Dame Sydney. All components are standardised (z-scored) before combining. The unweighted GAMSAT average means your three section scores are averaged without the 2:2:3 weighting ACER uses for the overall score.

Offer ranking formula

Offers are made based on a 50:50 combination of the pre-interview composite and the interview score. The pre-interview composite is the same 30:30:30:10 ranking described above.

The KCRMT Broome Program — Australia's most remote full MD

Kimberley Centre for Remote Medical Training

The KCRMT, which commenced in 2025, is a Commonwealth-funded, end-to-end 4-year MD program delivered entirely from Notre Dame's Broome campus. Up to 20 students complete all four years of medical training in regional WA, with clinical placements across the Kimberley and Pilbara regions in partnership with the Rural Clinical School of Western Australia (RCSWA).

This is not a rural stream bolted onto a metro program — it is a fully regional medical school. Students who receive a KCRMT place are based in Broome for the full four years of the program.

KCRMT applications are tiered. All KCRMT applicants must apply via GEMSAS (except Kimberley/Pilbara residents meeting the direct pathway) and list both the Fremantle and KCRMT Broome campuses in their preference order.

Tier 1
Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander applicants & equity applicants
Tier 1 applicants are ranked within their tier based on ATSI status and equity considerations. This tier has its own application window. Direct application to UNDA is available for Kimberley/Pilbara residents who have resided in the region for at least 5 years consecutively or 10 years cumulatively.
Direct application pathway for Kimberley/Pilbara long-term residents. ATSI students also eligible for Commonwealth-funded CSPs from 2026.
Tier 2
ATSI applicants or WA rural residents meeting the 10-year rule
Applicants who are ATSI from anywhere in Australia, or who have resided for at least 5 years consecutively or 10 years cumulatively in rural or regional areas and have 10+ cumulative years of WA residency. Must apply via GEMSAS with valid GAMSAT and CASPer.
Via GEMSAS. Ranked within tier using 30:30:30:10 formula. GAMSAT, CASPer and GPA all required.
Tier 3
Metropolitan or interstate applicants
Non-rural or non-WA-resident applicants who are interested in the KCRMT program. Must apply via GEMSAS. Preferencing the Fremantle campus may be taken into account when ranking for KCRMT interview and place offers.
Via GEMSAS. Competitive with rural and Tier 1/2 applicants. Fremantle preference noted in ranking.
Transfer between campuses is not expected. GEMSAS explicitly warns that transfer between Notre Dame's Fremantle, KCRMT Broome and Sydney campuses is granted only in exceptional circumstances. Apply for KCRMT only if you are genuinely willing and able to complete all four years in regional WA.

Facilitated interview pathways

UNDF offers facilitated interview pathways (FIPs) for specific undergraduate graduates who were not otherwise offered an interview through standard GEMSAS. FIP applicants must still meet all standard entry requirements and list Notre Dame Fremantle as their first preference.

Notre Dame undergraduate degree graduates
Graduates of any UNDA undergraduate degree (Fremantle or Sydney) who narrowly missed an interview in the standard GEMSAS pool may apply through the FIP. Completed or completing within the current or preceding year.
Requirements: GPA ≥6.0, unweighted GAMSAT ≥55; UNDF listed as first preference.
UNDA Graduate Diploma and Pre-Medicine Certificate graduates
UNDA Graduate Diploma of Health and Medical Sciences and Pre-Medicine Certificate graduates may also be eligible for a facilitated interview pathway. Check the Notre Dame Pathways to Medicine page for current requirements and application windows.
Requirements: meeting standard entry thresholds; UNDF listed as first preference. Check official UNDA page for exact criteria.

What applicants most commonly get wrong at Notre Dame Fremantle

❌
Treating CASPer as less important than GPA and GAMSAT
CASPer is 30% of your ranking — identical in weight to GPA and GAMSAT. UNDF is not a school where you can ignore CASPer and expect your academic scores to carry you. Invest meaningfully in CASPer preparation — it is the component you can most improve in a short time.
❌
Using the weighted GAMSAT instead of the unweighted average
Like Notre Dame Sydney, UNDF uses the unweighted average of S1, S2, S3 — not ACER's 2:2:3 weighted overall score. If your S3 is your strongest section, your ACER overall score will be higher than your Notre Dame unweighted average. Calculate the correct figure before assessing your competitiveness.
❌
Missing the WA residency bonus if you're a long-term WA resident
The WA residency bonus (10+ cumulative years in WA) is a Fremantle-specific advantage not available at other schools. If you qualify, make sure this is correctly flagged in your GEMSAS application — it contributes to your bonus points component.
❌
Not checking if not-for-degree study affects your GPA
UNDF (and UNDS) exclude not-for-degree study from GPA calculations. Exchange units, cross-institutional subjects and other not-for-degree study are excluded. This may change your Notre Dame GPA compared to other schools' calculations.
❌
Applying for KCRMT without understanding the full commitment
KCRMT places all students in Broome for four years. This is not a rural rotation or short placement — it is the entire program. Transfer is not expected to be granted. Only apply for KCRMT if you genuinely want to train in the Kimberley/Pilbara and commit to the WA rural health workforce.
❌
Missing the HDR deadline
HDR bonus points require your Masters by Research or PhD to be conferred by 31 July in the application year. An in-progress HDR, even one submitting in August, does not qualify for the bonus. Plan ahead if this is relevant to you.
