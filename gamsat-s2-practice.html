      About GAMSAT Section II — Task A & Task B Essay Writing

      GAMSAT Section II (Writing) requires candidates to write two essays — Task A and Task B — in 60 minutes. It accounts for one quarter of your overall GAMSAT score and is consistently the most underestimated section by Australian medical school applicants.

      Each task presents five stimulus comments grouped around a central theme. Candidates must identify the theme, select one or more comments to respond to, and produce a coherent, insightful piece of writing. You are not judged on the views you hold, but on the quality of your thinking and expression.

      Task A — Expository/Argumentative

      Typically socio-cultural in nature. Requires a clear thesis, logical argument, and evidence. Think: structured, persuasive essay with an analytical edge.

      Task B — Reflective/Discursive

      Personal and social themes. Can be narrative, reflective, or discursive. More latitude for voice and creativity — but still requires thematic depth.

      What examiners look for in GAMSAT Section II

      ACER assesses two criteria: Thought and Content (the quality and insightfulness of what you say) and Organisation and Expression (structure and language effectiveness). The major emphasis is on Thought and Content — which is why generic essay frameworks without genuine insight will plateau your score.

      High-scoring essays don't just summarise the stimulus quotes — they use them as a launching point for original, nuanced thinking. A Band 70+ response will often take an unexpected angle that still remains faithful to the theme, demonstrating independent thought rather than rote application of a framework.
