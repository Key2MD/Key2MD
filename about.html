The Tutor Behind the Results

Meet Dan

UniMelb MD student. Health economist. Former lecturer. The tutor who will help the real you shine — not turn you into a rehearsed robot.

Our Mission

Authenticity over mnemonics.

Key2MD is dedicated to providing comprehensive preparation for medical entrance exams in Australia, New Zealand, the UK, and globally — specialising in personalised 1:1 tutoring for GAMSAT Section II, CASPer, and Multiple Mini Interviews.

You won't get a cookie-cutter framework full of mnemonics that make you sound more rehearsed than ChatGPT. You will stand out with authenticity and empathy.

I cannot stress enough how many students get rejected each year for sounding rehearsed, or for receiving bad advice. With me, you get a unique approach that focuses on you being you and showing that in the best way possible — not simply answering with 'I'd speak to them 1:1 in a private room in a non-confrontational way', which signals to any interviewer that you're regurgitating buzzwords, not thinking as an individual.

Dan's Journey

A non-linear path to medicine.

Dan's story isn't a straight line — and that's exactly what makes him such an effective tutor.

🎓
High School → Early University
99.85 ATAR & Bachelor of Pre-Medical Science

After graduating high school, Dan achieved a 99.85 ATAR and went straight into a Bachelor of Pre Medical Science at Griffith University. Competing with the brightest students in QLD while studying all day at 19 was genuinely taxing — and Dan made a courageous decision to change course.

📊
Mid 20s
Economics Degree & Queensland Health

Dan completed a Bachelor of Economics at the University of Queensland and then worked at Queensland Health for several years as a health economist. Despite gaining rare insight into the healthcare system, the role didn't feel meaningful enough — so he decided to return to medicine.

🌏
Mid to Late 20s
Lecturing in China & Strategic Planning

With a low economics GPA and no clear path back to medicine, Dan spent a year lecturing at Sichuan University in China while planning his next move. He decided on an accelerated Bachelor of Psychology — completing it in 2 years by taking 5 courses per semester and every summer semester.

⭐
Age 28
6.99 GPA & Back on Track

Dan graduated with a 6.94 real GPA and 6.99 GEMSAS GPA. He sat the GAMSAT in his final year — largely unprepared, not wanting to risk his GPA — and scored 73/81/62. Happy with the result given he hadn't touched science in 9 years, he sat again in September with serious preparation and scored 75/83/79. He was offered CSP first-round at both University of Melbourne and University of Sydney.

🩺
Now
Clinical Years at UniMelb & Full-Time Tutoring

Dan is now in the clinical years of the MD at University of Melbourne, spending his free time tutoring students across all aspects of medical entrance. He's been tutoring CASPer and MMI for over 3 years, with success rates consistently in the 90%+ range. Every year he looks forward to the big day of offers — when students blow up his phone with screams (and tears) of joy.
