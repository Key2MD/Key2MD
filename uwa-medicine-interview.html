How UWA selects for medicine

UWA offers a graduate-entry Doctor of Medicine in Perth. Selection combines your GPA, the GAMSAT, and a multiple mini interview (MMI) for shortlisted applicants. UWA also runs a school-leaver assured/direct pathway (with ATAR and the UCAT), so check which pathway applies to you.

Official source: UWA Medicine admissions at uwa.edu.au.

What the UWA MMI tends to reward

In our coaching experience, UWA's MMI rewards clear, grounded, well-reasoned answers:

  • Sound reasoning under challenge - you adjust thoughtfully rather than freezing.
  • Professional judgement - sensible handling of safety, confidentiality and competing interests.
  • Awareness of rural and remote WA health when a station invites it.
  • Clear, human communication and genuine engagement in role-play stations.

Common mistakes we see

  • Generic answers that could fit any applicant.
  • Reaching for jargon instead of clear explanation.
  • Defending a first answer instead of thinking aloud when challenged.
  • Sounding rehearsed rather than genuine.

About the UWA MD program

UWA's Doctor of Medicine is a graduate-entry program of around four years, based in Perth with placements across WA including rural and remote sites. As a Group of Eight university, the curriculum is integrated and case-based with strong academic grounding. Confirm the current structure and pathways with UWA.

Fees (approximate)

Most domestic students study in a Commonwealth Supported Place (CSP) and pay the government-set annual student contribution for medicine - roughly AUD 10,000-11,000 per year as a 2025 guide, subject to change. Full-fee domestic and international places cost substantially more. For orientation only; confirm current fees with UWA.

Research opportunities

UWA offers research and honours-style options alongside the MD, with links to its medical research institutes. Confirm current options with UWA.

What students say (general perception)

The points below summarise common themes in public student discussion - opinions, not facts, and your experience may differ. Form your own view via open days and current students.

  • Commonly praised: a strong Group of Eight reputation, good clinical exposure, and an attractive Perth campus.
  • Commonly raised as challenges: Perth's distance from the eastern states, and rural placement travel.
