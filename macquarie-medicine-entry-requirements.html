Macquarie University · Graduate Medicine

Macquarie MD: full-fee, PhD-friendly, with reserved places for BCS graduates.

Macquarie is Australia's only full-fee graduate-entry MD — no CSPs exist. Its PhD GPA rule (automatic 7.0 weighting) and 50:50 ranking make it a strong option for PhD graduates with a lower undergraduate GPA. Three 3% GPA bonus cohorts apply, capped at 5%.

⚠ Full fee — no CSP places ✓ Min GPA: 5.0 (Macquarie weighted) ✓ Min GAMSAT: 50 overall + 50 each section ★ PhD → GPA treated as 7.0 BCS graduates: 10 reserved MMI places

MMI & Interview Lens

Macquarie MMI: global health, digital medicine, and campus-integrated care.

Macquarie pitches its MD as future-focused, globally connected and grounded in Australia's only university-integrated hospital. The program emphasises research-active patient care, simulation and robotic technology, and an international clinical reach. MMI answers should reflect innovation alongside safety, and cultural literacy across diverse health systems.

University-integrated hospitalMacquarie is the only Australian university with its own hospital on campus. This enables genuinely integrated clinical learning from early in the program. Frame this as an asset for patient-centred, research-adjacent clinical education.
Global clinical reachMacquarie has a formal partnership with Apollo Hospital in Hyderabad — one of India's largest hospital networks — enabling structured international clinical placements. Relevant if you have global health interests or experience.
Technology and simulationThe program promotes simulation spaces, robotic surgical systems and gamma knife radiotherapy access. For MMI, use these to discuss how technology supports — but doesn't replace — clinical judgment and patient communication.
Northern Sydney clinical ecosystemClinical placements also extend across Northern Sydney Local Health District, Northern Beaches Hospital and MindSpot Clinic. Demonstrate you understand what a distributed, technology-enabled clinical education looks like in practice.
Interview angle: Macquarie values applicants who are curious about healthcare innovation while remaining firmly patient-centred. Show you can navigate cultural diversity (Apollo partnership, multicultural Northern Sydney), think critically about digital health, and reflect on the cost and equity implications of full-fee medicine.
⚠ Macquarie MD — full fee only. This is the most important thing to understand before applying. Macquarie does not offer Commonwealth Supported Places. All domestic and international students pay full tuition fees — currently in the range of $50,000–$60,000 per year, totalling approximately $200,000–$240,000 for the 4-year program. This is a material financial decision and should factor into your preference ordering.

Ranking formula

Macquarie uses a 50:50 standardised composite of weighted GPA and overall GAMSAT for interview selection. Both components are standardised before combining — meaning your GPA and GAMSAT are each converted to z-scores relative to the applicant pool before being added together. This is different from a simple percentage-weighted average.

Competitive note: The 5.0 minimum GPA is the floor, not the target. The 2024 intake averaged 6.7 GPA and 66 GAMSAT across sections. A 5.0 GPA is unlikely to be competitive for an interview offer in the standard pool.

How Macquarie calculates your GPA

Macquarie uses its own GPA weighting formula — not the standard GEMSAS weighted formula used by most other schools. It weights more recent and higher-level study more heavily. Understanding this is critical: your Macquarie GPA may be meaningfully different from your GEMSAS GPA at other schools.

GPA bonus cohorts — capped at 5% total

Macquarie applies a 3% bonus to the weighted GPA for each listed cohort. If you are eligible for more than one, the total bonus is capped at 5% — you cannot receive 6% or 9% for multiple cohorts. The bonus is applied before standardisation for the ranking formula. Bonuses are not available to international applicants.

5% cap is the most commonly missed rule. ATSI + Rural = 5% (not 6%). ATSI + BCS = 5% (not 6%). Rural + BCS = 5% (not 6%). All three = 5% (not 9%). The cap applies regardless of how many cohorts you belong to.

Bachelor of Clinical Science reserved MMI places

Beyond the 3% GPA bonus, Macquarie reserves 10 MMI places specifically for Macquarie Bachelor of Clinical Science graduates or final-year students. This is a pathway advantage, not just a marginal GPA uplift: BCS students compete in a smaller reserved pool for those 10 places, rather than against the full applicant field for all places. If you are a current or recent MQ BCS student, this makes Macquarie a particularly natural preference.

What applicants most commonly get wrong at Macquarie

❌
Underestimating the cost
The full-fee structure is not a small consideration. Macquarie MD is approximately $200,000–$240,000 in tuition. Before preferencing Macquarie, understand the FEE-HELP implications and how HECS debt differs from FEE-HELP debt for repayment.
❌
Using the wrong GPA formula
Macquarie uses its own weighting formula, not the standard GEMSAS GPA. Your GPA at Macquarie may be meaningfully higher or lower than your GPA at Melbourne, ANU or UQ. Run the Macquarie-specific calculation before assessing competitiveness.
❌
Claiming more than 5% bonus
Applicants who are both rural and ATSI sometimes expect a 6% combined bonus. The cap is 5% — this is clearly stated in the GEMSAS guide. The calculator applies the 5% cap regardless of how many bonus cohorts are selected.
❌
Not understanding the PhD GPA treatment
A completed PhD means the postgraduate research component is weighted ×3 and capped at GPA 7.0 in Macquarie's formula. This is not a blanket "your GPA becomes 7.0" — your overall Macquarie GPA depends on the weighted combination with your bachelor results. But it is a substantial uplift for PhD graduates with a lower undergraduate GPA.
❌
Thinking the minimum 5.0 GPA is competitive
The 2024 cohort averaged 6.7 GPA and 66 GAMSAT per section. A 5.0 GPA meets the eligibility floor, not the competitive threshold. Macquarie is genuinely competitive — its full-fee status does not make it easier to enter.
❌
Ignoring the BCS reserved places if you're a MQ student
If you are a Macquarie BCS student or graduate, the 10 reserved MMI places represent a meaningful structural advantage that is separate from the 3% GPA bonus. This pathway should be front-of-mind when choosing undergraduate study or assessing competitiveness.
