How Melbourne selects for the MD

The University of Melbourne offers a graduate-entry Doctor of Medicine. Selection combines your GPA, the GAMSAT, and a multiple mini interview (MMI) for shortlisted applicants.

Official source: University of Melbourne MD admissions at study.unimelb.edu.au.

What the Melbourne MMI tends to reward

As a graduate program, Melbourne's MMI expects maturity and depth. In our coaching experience the strongest answers show:

  • Mature reasoning - you hold complexity and uncertainty without collapsing into a black-and-white answer.
  • Reflective insight - honest learning from real experience.
  • Professional judgement - sensible handling of safety, confidentiality and competing interests.
  • Clear, composed communication under time pressure.

Common mistakes we see

  • Surface-level reflection with no specific, honest detail.
  • Rushing to a conclusion before weighing the perspectives involved.
  • Sounding rehearsed - answers that break under a follow-up.
  • Treating role-play stations as a monologue rather than a conversation.

About the Melbourne MD program

Melbourne's Doctor of Medicine is a graduate-entry program of around four years, based in Melbourne with clinical placements across its teaching-hospital network. As a research-intensive Group of Eight university, the curriculum is case-based with strong academic and research grounding. Confirm the current structure with Melbourne.

Fees (approximate)

Most domestic students study in a Commonwealth Supported Place (CSP) and pay the government-set annual student contribution for medicine - roughly AUD 10,000-11,000 per year as a 2025 guide, subject to change. Full-fee domestic and international places cost substantially more. For orientation only; confirm current fees with Melbourne.

Research opportunities

Melbourne is a research powerhouse: the MD includes a scholarly research component, and there are strong links to major medical research institutes for students who want to go deeper. Confirm current options with Melbourne.

What students say (general perception)

The points below summarise common themes in public student discussion - opinions, not facts, and your experience may differ. Form your own view via open days and current students.

  • Commonly praised: strong prestige and research opportunities, and excellent teaching hospitals.
  • Commonly raised as challenges: a high cost of living in Melbourne and a competitive, demanding environment.
